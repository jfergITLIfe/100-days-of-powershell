Let me be straight with you: most PowerShell content is either too shallow to be useful or too dense to finish. You either get a list of commands with no context, or a 400-page book that kills your momentum by chapter three.

This challenge is neither.

100 days. One concept per day. 5–10 minutes. Working code every single session. By the end, you'll have five real projects on your GitHub and the scripting instincts to handle whatever the job throws at you.

---

## Who this is for

- IT help desk professionals who want to level up
- Sysadmins who've been avoiding PowerShell
- Security analysts who want to automate their workflow
- Anyone who's started and quit a PowerShell course before

You don't need any prior PowerShell experience. You do need a Windows machine and the willingness to open a terminal.

---

## How each day works

Every day follows the same format:

- **Overview** — the concept in plain English, and why it matters on the job
- **The code** — working examples you run yourself, right now
- **What you learned** — a quick summary to lock it in
- **Challenge** — one thing to do to prove you got it

That's it. No videos to watch. No quizzes. Just you and a terminal.

---

## The five phases

| Phase | Days | What you're building toward |
|-------|------|----------------------------|
| 1 — Foundations | 001–020 | Comfortable in the shell, writing basic scripts |
| 2 — Scripting Core | 021–040 | Real logic, loops, functions, error handling |
| 3 — Files, Systems & Data | 041–060 | Managing systems at scale |
| 4 — Automation & Real-World Tools | 061–080 | AD, Azure, APIs, job-ready automation |
| 5 — Security Pipeline | 081–100 | Threat hunting, IR triage, defensive tooling |

Each phase ends with a **capstone project** — a real, submittable script you'll build from scratch and push to your own GitHub fork.

---

## How to submit your work

### Daily challenges
Each day has a small challenge. Don't skip it — that's where the learning actually locks in. Post your result (a screenshot or GitHub Gist link) in [GitHub Discussions → Daily Submissions](https://github.com/jfergitlife/100-days-of-powershell/discussions/categories/daily-submissions).

### Capstone projects
Days 20, 40, 60, 80, and 100 are full projects. Use the [Capstone Submission Template](https://github.com/jfergitlife/100-days-of-powershell/blob/main/CAPSTONE_SUBMISSION_TEMPLATE.md) — it walks you through exactly what to include.

### The showcase wall
Finish a capstone and want to be listed publicly? Submit a small PR to add yourself to `showcases.json`. Your fork, your project, your name — on the wall permanently.

## Before you start

1. **Fork this repo** on GitHub — that fork is your personal challenge record
2. Make sure you have **PowerShell 7+** installed ([download here](https://github.com/PowerShell/PowerShell/releases))
3. Open **VS Code** with the PowerShell extension, or use the built-in PowerShell terminal
4. Head to [Day 001 →](./phase-1/day-001)
